About Me

2023-04-14

Hello, I'm Franklin! I'm a sophomore at Dublin High School, and I'm passionate about programming, writing and math.

I have a long history with STEM; ever since first grade I have loved math, and I took up programming during quarantine. I have spent countless hours studying math (both for competitions and for fun), and hacking on personal programming projects. You can check them out on my Projects page. From these experiences I've learned a lot about what is necessary to be a good programmer or mathematician: curiosity, persistence, and a willingness to endlessly Google.

I'm also pursuing STEM in school. In my freshman year, I took CSE (Computer Science Essentials), which taught basic programming skills. This year I'm taking AP CSP (Computer Science Principles), which touches on various aspects of computing, from program design to cybersecurity to social impacts.

I hope to build more on what I have learned, by trying new and more challenging things. I'd like to do this through my own personal projects; I have plenty of ideas on what to do, including games, contributions to the Linux kernel, and just fixing annoyances in computing I encounter every day. I'd also like to expand my knowledge by going to a technical school such as MIT, Caltech of Berkeley. At this level, I would have the skills and platform to share what I've learned and use it to help others.
